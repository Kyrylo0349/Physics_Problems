# Task 09 – Momentum Comparison

## Problem Statement

Compare the momenta of:

- a fly of mass $2$ g moving at $10$ m/s,
- a tennis ball of mass $60$ g moving at $1$ m/s.

Determine which has the greater momentum.

## Theory

Linear momentum is defined by

$$
p = mv
$$

where $m$ is mass and $v$ is velocity.

To compare momenta correctly, masses must first be converted to SI units.

## Step-by-Step Solution

### 1. Fly

Convert the mass:

$$
2 \text{ g} = 0.002 \text{ kg}
$$

Then

$$
p_{\text{fly}} = 0.002 \cdot 10 = 0.020 \text{ kg m/s}
$$

### 2. Tennis ball

Convert the mass:

$$
60 \text{ g} = 0.060 \text{ kg}
$$

Then

$$
p_{\text{ball}} = 0.060 \cdot 1 = 0.060 \text{ kg m/s}
$$

### 3. Comparison

Compare the two values:

$$
0.060 > 0.020
$$

So the tennis ball has the larger momentum.

## Final Result

The tennis ball has greater momentum.

Its momentum is

$$
0.060 \text{ kg m/s}
$$

while the fly has momentum

$$
0.020 \text{ kg m/s}
$$

## Interpretation

Although the fly moves much faster, its mass is very small. Momentum depends on both mass and velocity, and in this case the larger mass of the tennis ball dominates the comparison.
