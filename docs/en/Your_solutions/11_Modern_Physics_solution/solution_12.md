# Task 12 – Beta Decay

## Problem Statement

Give a specific balanced beta-minus decay equation starting with Carbon-14,

$$
{}^{14}_{6}\text{C}
$$

## Theory

In beta-minus decay, a neutron turns into a proton, an electron, and an electron antineutrino.

Therefore:

- the mass number stays the same,
- the atomic number increases by 1.

## Step-by-Step Solution

Starting from

$$
{}^{14}_{6}\text{C}
$$

after beta-minus decay, the daughter nucleus has

$$
A = 14
$$

and

$$
Z = 7
$$

Element

$$
Z = 7
$$

is nitrogen.

## Final Result

The balanced beta-minus decay equation is

$$
{}^{14}_{6}\text{C} \to {}^{14}_{7}\text{N} + e^- + \bar{\nu}_e
$$

## Interpretation

In beta-minus decay, the nucleus becomes one element higher in atomic number because one neutron is converted into a proton.
