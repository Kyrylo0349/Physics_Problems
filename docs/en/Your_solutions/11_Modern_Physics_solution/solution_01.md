# Task 01 – De Broglie Wavelength

## Problem Statement

Calculate:

- the de Broglie wavelength of an electron with kinetic energy

$$
K = 150 \text{ eV}
$$

- the de Broglie wavelength of a golf ball of mass

$$
m = 50 \text{ g}
$$

moving at speed

$$
v = 60 \text{ m/s}
$$

Then explain why quantum effects are not noticeable for macroscopic objects.

## Theory

The de Broglie wavelength is

$$
\lambda = \frac{h}{p}
$$

For a non-relativistic particle,

$$
p = \sqrt{2mK}
$$

or, if the mass and speed are known directly,

$$
p = mv
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

### 1. Electron with kinetic energy 150 eV

Convert the kinetic energy to joules:

$$
K = 150 \cdot 1.602 \times 10^{-19} \text{ J}
$$

$$
K \approx 2.40 \times 10^{-17} \text{ J}
$$

Now calculate the momentum:

$$
p = \sqrt{2m_e K}
$$

Using

$$
m_e = 9.11 \times 10^{-31} \text{ kg}
$$

gives

$$
p \approx 6.62 \times 10^{-24} \text{ kg m/s}
$$

Therefore,

$$
\lambda = \frac{6.626 \times 10^{-34}}{6.62 \times 10^{-24}}
$$

$$
\lambda \approx 1.00 \times 10^{-10} \text{ m}
$$

This is

$$
\lambda \approx 0.100 \text{ nm}
$$

### 2. Golf ball

Convert the mass:

$$
m = 50 \text{ g} = 0.050 \text{ kg}
$$

Momentum:

$$
p = mv = 0.050 \cdot 60 = 3.0 \text{ kg m/s}
$$

Then

$$
\lambda = \frac{h}{p} = \frac{6.626 \times 10^{-34}}{3.0}
$$

$$
\lambda \approx 2.21 \times 10^{-34} \text{ m}
$$

### 3. Why macroscopic quantum effects are not noticeable

For macroscopic objects, the de Broglie wavelength is extremely small. It is far smaller than atomic dimensions and any practical measurement scale, so wave-like effects such as diffraction and interference are unobservable in ordinary conditions.

## Final Result

Electron:

$$
\lambda \approx 1.00 \times 10^{-10} \text{ m} = 0.100 \text{ nm}
$$

Golf ball:

$$
\lambda \approx 2.21 \times 10^{-34} \text{ m}
$$

Quantum effects are not noticeable for macroscopic objects because their de Broglie wavelengths are extraordinarily small.

## Interpretation

The electron wavelength is comparable to atomic spacing, which is why electron diffraction is observable. The golf ball wavelength is so tiny that its wave nature is completely negligible in practice.
