# Task 10 – Radioactive Half-Life

## Problem Statement

Cobalt-60 has half-life

$$
T_{1/2} = 5.27 \text{ years}
$$

If a sample initially contains

$$
100 \text{ g}
$$

how much remains after approximately

$$
21 \text{ years}
$$

## Theory

Radioactive decay follows

$$
N = N_0 \left(\frac{1}{2}\right)^{t/T_{1/2}}
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

Substitute the values:

$$
N = 100 \left(\frac{1}{2}\right)^{21/5.27}
$$

First compute the exponent:

$$
\frac{21}{5.27} \approx 3.98
$$

Then

$$
N \approx 100 \left(\frac{1}{2}\right)^{3.98}
$$

$$
N \approx 6.32 \text{ g}
$$

This is very close to four half-lives, which would give

$$
100 \to 50 \to 25 \to 12.5 \to 6.25 \text{ g}
$$

## Final Result

After 21 years, the remaining mass is approximately

$$
6.3 \text{ g}
$$

## Interpretation

Since 21 years is almost four half-lives, only a small fraction of the original sample remains.
