# Task 07 – Hydrogen Emission Spectra

## Problem Statement

A hydrogen atom undergoes a transition from

$$
n = 4
$$

to

$$
n = 2
$$

Determine:

- the photon energy,
- the wavelength,
- the color of the emitted light.

Use

$$
E_n = -\frac{13.6 \text{ eV}}{n^2}
$$

## Theory

The emitted photon energy is the difference between the two energy levels:

$$
E_{\gamma} = E_i - E_f
$$

The wavelength is found from

$$
\lambda = \frac{hc}{E_{\gamma}}
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

### 1. Initial and final energies

For

$$
n = 4
$$

$$
E_4 = -\frac{13.6}{16} = -0.85 \text{ eV}
$$

For

$$
n = 2
$$

$$
E_2 = -\frac{13.6}{4} = -3.40 \text{ eV}
$$

### 2. Photon energy

$$
E_{\gamma} = E_4 - E_2
$$

$$
E_{\gamma} = (-0.85) - (-3.40)
$$

$$
E_{\gamma} = 2.55 \text{ eV}
$$

### 3. Wavelength

Use

$$
\lambda = \frac{1240 \text{ eV nm}}{2.55 \text{ eV}}
$$

$$
\lambda \approx 486 \text{ nm}
$$

### 4. Color

A wavelength near

$$
486 \text{ nm}
$$

lies in the blue-green region of the visible spectrum.

## Final Result

The emitted photon energy is

$$
E_{\gamma} = 2.55 \text{ eV}
$$

The wavelength is

$$
\lambda \approx 486 \text{ nm}
$$

The light is

$$
\text{blue-green}
$$

## Interpretation

This is one of the Balmer-series lines of hydrogen, which lie in the visible range and are important in spectroscopy.
