# Task 11 – Alpha Decay

## Problem Statement

Give a specific balanced nuclear equation for an alpha decay process starting with Uranium-238,

$$
{}^{238}_{92}\text{U}
$$

## Theory

In alpha decay, the nucleus emits an alpha particle,

$$
{}^{4}_{2}\text{He}
$$

Therefore:

- the mass number decreases by 4,
- the atomic number decreases by 2.

## Step-by-Step Solution

Starting from

$$
{}^{238}_{92}\text{U}
$$

after alpha decay, the daughter nucleus has

$$
A = 238 - 4 = 234
$$

and

$$
Z = 92 - 2 = 90
$$

Element

$$
Z = 90
$$

is thorium.

## Final Result

The balanced alpha-decay equation is

$$
{}^{238}_{92}\text{U} \to {}^{234}_{90}\text{Th} + {}^{4}_{2}\text{He}
$$

## Interpretation

Alpha decay lowers both the mass number and the charge of the nucleus because a helium nucleus is emitted.
