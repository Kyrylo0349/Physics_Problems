# Task 06 – Quantum Numbers for n = 3

## Problem Statement

For the hydrogen atom level

$$
n = 3
$$

determine the possible values of the quantum numbers

$$
l
$$

and

$$
m_l
$$

How many distinct electron states exist for

$$
n = 3
$$

## Theory

For a given principal quantum number $n$:

$$
l = 0, 1, 2, \dots, n-1
$$

For each orbital quantum number $l$:

$$
m_l = -l, -(l-1), \dots, 0, \dots, l-1, l
$$

The number of spatial states for a given $n$ is

$$
n^2
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

For

$$
n = 3
$$

the allowed values of $l$ are

$$
l = 0, 1, 2
$$

### 1. For l = 0

$$
m_l = 0
$$

### 2. For l = 1

$$
m_l = -1, 0, 1
$$

### 3. For l = 2

$$
m_l = -2, -1, 0, 1, 2
$$

The total number of spatial states is

$$
1 + 3 + 5 = 9
$$

## Final Result

For

$$
n = 3
$$

the allowed values are:

- for

$$
l = 0
$$

$$
m_l = 0
$$

- for

$$
l = 1
$$

$$
m_l = -1, 0, 1
$$

- for

$$
l = 2
$$

$$
m_l = -2, -1, 0, 1, 2
$$

The total number of distinct spatial electron states is

$$
9
$$

## Interpretation

The higher the value of $n$, the more orbital patterns are available. For

$$
n = 3
$$

there are three subshells and nine spatial states in total.
