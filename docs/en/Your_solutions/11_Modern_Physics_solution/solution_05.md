# Task 05 – Heisenberg Uncertainty Principle

## Problem Statement

Using

$$
\Delta x \Delta p \ge \frac{\hbar}{2}
$$

find the minimum uncertainty in the velocity of an electron confined to a region of width

$$
\Delta x = 0.1 \text{ nm}
$$

## Theory

Since momentum is

$$
p = mv
$$

the uncertainty relation becomes

$$
\Delta x \, m \Delta v \ge \frac{\hbar}{2}
$$

Thus,

$$
\Delta v \ge \frac{\hbar}{2m\Delta x}
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

Convert the position uncertainty:

$$
\Delta x = 0.1 \text{ nm} = 1.0 \times 10^{-10} \text{ m}
$$

Now use

$$
\Delta v \ge \frac{\hbar}{2m_e \Delta x}
$$

with

$$
\hbar = 1.055 \times 10^{-34} \text{ J s}
$$

and

$$
m_e = 9.11 \times 10^{-31} \text{ kg}
$$

Substituting gives

$$
\Delta v \ge \frac{1.055 \times 10^{-34}}{2(9.11 \times 10^{-31})(1.0 \times 10^{-10})}
$$

$$
\Delta v \ge 5.79 \times 10^5 \text{ m/s}
$$

## Final Result

The minimum uncertainty in velocity is

$$
\Delta v_{\min} \approx 5.8 \times 10^5 \text{ m/s}
$$

## Interpretation

Confined particles must have large momentum uncertainty. This is why electrons in atoms cannot be treated as tiny classical particles with precise position and velocity at the same time.
