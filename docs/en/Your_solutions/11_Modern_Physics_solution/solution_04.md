# Task 04 – Radius and Angular Momentum in the Bohr Model

## Problem Statement

Using the Bohr model, determine the orbital radius and angular momentum of the electron in hydrogen for the state

$$
n = 2
$$

Express the radius in meters and in units of the Bohr radius

$$
a_0
$$

and express the angular momentum in units of

$$
\hbar
$$

## Theory

In the Bohr model,

$$
r_n = n^2 a_0
$$

and

$$
L_n = n\hbar
$$

The Bohr radius is approximately

$$
a_0 \approx 5.29 \times 10^{-11} \text{ m}
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

### 1. Orbital radius

For

$$
n = 2
$$

$$
r_2 = 2^2 a_0 = 4a_0
$$

Using the numerical value of $a_0$:

$$
r_2 = 4 \cdot 5.29 \times 10^{-11} \text{ m}
$$

$$
r_2 \approx 2.12 \times 10^{-10} \text{ m}
$$

### 2. Angular momentum

$$
L_2 = 2\hbar
$$

## Final Result

The orbital radius is

$$
r_2 = 4a_0 \approx 2.12 \times 10^{-10} \text{ m}
$$

The angular momentum is

$$
L_2 = 2\hbar
$$

## Interpretation

In the Bohr model, both orbital radius and angular momentum are quantized. Higher energy levels correspond to larger orbits and larger angular momentum.
