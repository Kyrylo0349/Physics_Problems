# Task 08 – Quantum Well Spectra

## Problem Statement

An electron is in a one-dimensional infinite potential well of width

$$
L = 0.5 \text{ nm}
$$

Determine the wavelength of the photon emitted when the electron transitions from

$$
n = 4
$$

to

$$
n = 2
$$

## Theory

For a one-dimensional infinite potential well,

$$
E_n = \frac{n^2 h^2}{8mL^2}
$$

The emitted photon energy is

$$
E_{\gamma} = E_4 - E_2
$$

and the wavelength is

$$
\lambda = \frac{hc}{E_{\gamma}}
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

The energy difference is

$$
E_{\gamma} = \frac{(4^2 - 2^2)h^2}{8mL^2}
$$

$$
E_{\gamma} = \frac{12h^2}{8mL^2}
$$

$$
E_{\gamma} = \frac{3h^2}{2mL^2}
$$

Using

$$
m = m_e
$$

and

$$
L = 0.5 \times 10^{-9} \text{ m}
$$

gives

$$
E_{\gamma} \approx 18.05 \text{ eV}
$$

Now calculate the wavelength:

$$
\lambda = \frac{1240 \text{ eV nm}}{18.05 \text{ eV}}
$$

$$
\lambda \approx 68.7 \text{ nm}
$$

## Final Result

The emitted photon wavelength is approximately

$$
\lambda \approx 68.7 \text{ nm}
$$

## Interpretation

This wavelength lies in the ultraviolet region. Quantum confinement in a very small well produces large energy spacing between levels.
