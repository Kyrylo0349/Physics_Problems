# Task 09 – Pair Annihilation

## Problem Statement

An electron and a positron, each with rest mass

$$
0.511 \text{ MeV}/c^2
$$

annihilate to produce two photons of equal energy.

Determine the energy and wavelength of each photon.

## Theory

If the electron and positron annihilate from rest, their total rest energy is

$$
2m_ec^2
$$

This energy is shared equally by the two photons.

Thus each photon has energy

$$
E_{\gamma} = 0.511 \text{ MeV}
$$

The wavelength is

$$
\lambda = \frac{hc}{E}
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

### 1. Photon energy

Each photon receives half the total energy:

$$
E_{\gamma} = 0.511 \text{ MeV}
$$

### 2. Wavelength

Use the equivalent form

$$
\lambda = \frac{1240 \text{ eV nm}}{511000 \text{ eV}}
$$

$$
\lambda \approx 0.00243 \text{ nm}
$$

This is also

$$
\lambda \approx 2.43 \times 10^{-12} \text{ m}
$$

## Final Result

Each photon has energy

$$
E_{\gamma} = 0.511 \text{ MeV}
$$

and wavelength

$$
\lambda \approx 2.43 \times 10^{-12} \text{ m}
$$

## Interpretation

Electron-positron annihilation produces gamma rays. The resulting photons are very energetic and therefore have extremely short wavelengths.
