# Task 02 – X-Ray Production

## Problem Statement

What is the minimum potential difference needed in an X-ray tube to produce X-rays with wavelength

$$
\lambda = 0.01 \text{ nm}
$$

## Theory

The photon energy is

$$
E = hf = \frac{hc}{\lambda}
$$

In an X-ray tube, the maximum photon energy comes from an electron accelerated through voltage $V$:

$$
E = qV
$$

Thus,

$$
V = \frac{hc}{q\lambda}
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

Convert the wavelength:

$$
\lambda = 0.01 \text{ nm} = 1.0 \times 10^{-11} \text{ m}
$$

Now use

$$
V = \frac{hc}{e\lambda}
$$

Substitute the constants:

$$
V = \frac{(6.626 \times 10^{-34})(3.00 \times 10^8)}{(1.602 \times 10^{-19})(1.0 \times 10^{-11})}
$$

$$
V \approx 1.24 \times 10^5 \text{ V}
$$

## Final Result

The minimum potential difference is approximately

$$
V \approx 1.24 \times 10^5 \text{ V}
$$

or

$$
124 \text{ kV}
$$

## Interpretation

Producing very short-wavelength X-rays requires very energetic electrons, which means a very large accelerating voltage.
