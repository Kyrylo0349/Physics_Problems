# Task 03 – Photoelectric Effect

## Problem Statement

Potassium has work function

$$
\phi = 2.3 \text{ eV}
$$

It is illuminated with light of wavelength

$$
\lambda = 350 \text{ nm}
$$

Determine the maximum kinetic energy of the emitted electrons.

## Theory

Einstein's photoelectric equation is

$$
K_{\max} = hf - \phi = \frac{hc}{\lambda} - \phi
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

First calculate the photon energy in electronvolts:

$$
E_{\gamma} = \frac{1240 \text{ eV nm}}{350 \text{ nm}}
$$

$$
E_{\gamma} \approx 3.54 \text{ eV}
$$

Now subtract the work function:

$$
K_{\max} = 3.54 - 2.3
$$

$$
K_{\max} \approx 1.24 \text{ eV}
$$

## Final Result

The maximum kinetic energy is

$$
K_{\max} \approx 1.24 \text{ eV}
$$

## Interpretation

The photon energy is larger than the work function, so photoemission occurs. The excess energy appears as the kinetic energy of the ejected electron.
