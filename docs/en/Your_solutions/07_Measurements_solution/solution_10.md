# Task 10 – Measuring the Speed of Light with a Microwave Oven

## Problem Statement

Use a microwave oven, a bar of chocolate or slices of cheese, and a ruler to estimate the speed of light.

The microwave frequency is

$$
f = 2.45 \text{ GHz}
$$

Compare the measured result with

$$
c = 3.00 \times 10^8 \text{ m/s}
$$

and calculate the percentage error.

## Theory

Inside the microwave oven, a standing electromagnetic wave forms. The melted spots correspond approximately to antinodes.

The distance between adjacent melted spots is half a wavelength:

$$
d = \frac{\lambda}{2}
$$

so

$$
\lambda = 2d
$$

Then the speed of light is calculated from

$$
c_{\text{meas}} = f\lambda
$$

Since

$$
f = 2.45 \times 10^9 \text{ Hz}
$$

we get

$$
c_{\text{meas}} = 2.45 \times 10^9 \cdot 2d
$$

The percentage error is

$$
\%\text{ error} = \left| \frac{c_{\text{meas}} - c}{c} \right| \cdot 100\%
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

### 1. Measure the spacing between melted spots

Let the measured distance between two neighboring melted spots be

$$
d
$$

### 2. Determine the wavelength

$$
\lambda = 2d
$$

### 3. Calculate the measured speed of light

$$
c_{\text{meas}} = f\lambda = 2.45 \times 10^9 \cdot 2d
$$

### 4. Compare with the accepted value

Use

$$
c = 3.00 \times 10^8 \text{ m/s}
$$

and compute

$$
\%\text{ error} = \left| \frac{c_{\text{meas}} - 3.00 \times 10^8}{3.00 \times 10^8} \right| \cdot 100\%
$$

## Final Result

The numerical result depends on the measured value of

$$
d
$$

The final report should be written as

$$
\lambda = 2d
$$

$$
c_{\text{meas}} = 2.45 \times 10^9 \cdot 2d
$$

followed by the percentage error.

## Interpretation

This experiment works because the microwave oven produces standing waves, and the spacing of melted regions gives direct access to the wavelength. The result is approximate, but it usually gives the right order of magnitude for the speed of light.
