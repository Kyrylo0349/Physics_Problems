# Task 04 – Relative Uncertainty

## Problem Statement

A car speedometer has an uncertainty of

$$
5\%
$$

If it reads

$$
60 \text{ km/h}
$$

what is the range of the actual speed?

## Theory

A percentage uncertainty gives the absolute uncertainty through

$$
\Delta x = \frac{p}{100}x
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

Calculate the absolute uncertainty:

$$
\Delta v = 0.05 \cdot 60 = 3 \text{ km/h}
$$

So the actual speed lies in the interval

$$
60 - 3 = 57 \text{ km/h}
$$

to

$$
60 + 3 = 63 \text{ km/h}
$$

## Final Result

The actual speed is in the range

$$
57 \text{ km/h} \leq v \leq 63 \text{ km/h}
$$

## Interpretation

A 5% uncertainty means the true value may differ from the displayed one by 5% of the reading.
