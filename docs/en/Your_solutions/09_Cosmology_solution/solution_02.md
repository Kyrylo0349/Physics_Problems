# Task 02 – Cosmic Temperatures

## Problem Statement

Order the following temperatures from hottest to coldest:

- core of the Sun
- surface of the Sun
- present Cosmic Microwave Background
- interstellar space
- temperature of the Universe at the nucleosynthesis era
- human body temperature
- temperature of the Universe at the recombination era
- temperature of a black hole with 10 solar masses
- ionization temperature of hydrogen

## Theory

Only approximate ordering is required. Typical reference scales are:

- nucleosynthesis era: about $10^9 \text{ K}$
- core of the Sun: about $1.5 \times 10^7 \text{ K}$
- ionization temperature of hydrogen from $13.6 \text{ eV}$: about $1.58 \times 10^5 \text{ K}$
- surface of the Sun: about $5800 \text{ K}$
- recombination era: about $3000 \text{ K}$
- human body: about $310 \text{ K}$
- interstellar space: typically tens of kelvin in a simple textbook estimate
- present CMB: about $2.7 \text{ K}$
- a 10-solar-mass black hole: extremely cold, about $10^{-9} \text{ K}$

## Step-by-Step Solution

From hottest to coldest, the ordering is:

1. temperature of the Universe at the nucleosynthesis era
2. core of the Sun
3. ionization temperature of hydrogen
4. surface of the Sun
5. temperature of the Universe at the recombination era
6. human body temperature
7. interstellar space
8. present Cosmic Microwave Background
9. temperature of a black hole with 10 solar masses

## Final Result

The correct order is

$$
\text{nucleosynthesis era} \rightarrow \text{Sun core} \rightarrow \text{hydrogen ionization temperature} \rightarrow \text{Sun surface} \rightarrow \text{recombination era} \rightarrow \text{human body} \rightarrow \text{interstellar space} \rightarrow \text{present CMB} \rightarrow \text{10-solar-mass black hole}
$$

## Interpretation

The early universe was far hotter than present-day astrophysical objects. Stellar interiors are extremely hot, everyday temperatures are much lower, and Hawking temperatures of stellar-mass black holes are extraordinarily tiny.
