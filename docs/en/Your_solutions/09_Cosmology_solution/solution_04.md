# Task 04a – Hubble's Law

## Problem Statement

According to Hubble's law,

$$
v = H_0 d
$$

and using

$$
H_0 \approx 70 \text{ (km/s)/Mpc}
$$

determine the distance at which a galaxy would be receding from us at the speed of light.

## Theory

Set

$$
v = c
$$

and solve for the distance:

$$
d = \frac{c}{H_0}
$$

Using

$$
c \approx 3.00 \times 10^5 \text{ km/s}
$$

gives the Hubble distance.

## Step-by-Step Solution

Substitute the values:

$$
d = \frac{3.00 \times 10^5}{70} \text{ Mpc}
$$

$$
d \approx 4285.7 \text{ Mpc}
$$

Convert to gigaparsecs:

$$
d \approx 4.29 \text{ Gpc}
$$

Using

$$
1 \text{ Mpc} \approx 3.26 \times 10^6 \text{ ly}
$$

gives

$$
d \approx 4285.7 \cdot 3.26 \times 10^6 \text{ ly}
$$

$$
d \approx 1.40 \times 10^{10} \text{ ly}
$$

## Final Result

The distance is approximately

$$
d \approx 4286 \text{ Mpc} \approx 4.29 \text{ Gpc} \approx 14.0 \text{ billion light-years}
$$

## Interpretation

This result is the Hubble distance obtained from the simple linear law

$$
v = H_0 d
$$

It is not the same as the full present-day radius of the observable universe.




# Task 04b – Galactic Year

## Problem Statement

Our Solar System orbits the center of the Milky Way at a distance of about

$$
27000 \text{ light-years}
$$

with speed

$$
220 \text{ km/s}
$$

Determine how long it takes to complete one full orbit.

## Theory

For circular motion, the orbital period is

$$
T = \frac{2\pi r}{v}
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

Convert the orbital radius to kilometers:

$$
r = 27000 \times 9.4607 \times 10^{12} \text{ km}
$$

$$
r \approx 2.55 \times 10^{17} \text{ km}
$$

Now compute the orbital circumference:

$$
2\pi r \approx 1.60 \times 10^{18} \text{ km}
$$

Then the period is

$$
T = \frac{1.60 \times 10^{18}}{220} \text{ s}
$$

$$
T \approx 7.30 \times 10^{15} \text{ s}
$$

Convert to years:

$$
T \approx \frac{7.30 \times 10^{15}}{365.25 \times 24 \times 3600}
$$

$$
T \approx 2.31 \times 10^8 \text{ years}
$$

## Final Result

A galactic year is approximately

$$
T \approx 2.31 \times 10^8 \text{ years}
$$

or about

$$
231 \text{ million years}
$$

## Interpretation

The Solar System moves rapidly, but the orbit around the galactic center is enormous, so one full revolution takes hundreds of millions of years.
