# Task 06 – Galactic Communication

## Problem Statement

Estimate how long it would take to exchange mutual greetings between two civilizations located at opposite ends of the Milky Way, assuming they communicate at the speed of light.

## Theory

Take the diameter of the Milky Way to be approximately

$$
100000 \text{ light-years}
$$

Because one light-year is the distance that light travels in one year, the time in years is numerically equal to the distance in light-years.

## Step-by-Step Solution

A one-way message across the Milky Way takes

$$
100000 \text{ years}
$$

If the other civilization replies immediately, the return signal also takes

$$
100000 \text{ years}
$$

So the total time for mutual greetings is

$$
200000 \text{ years}
$$

## Final Result

The one-way communication time is

$$
100000 \text{ years}
$$

The round-trip time for mutual greetings is

$$
200000 \text{ years}
$$

## Interpretation

Even light-speed communication is extremely slow on galactic scales. A simple exchange of greetings would take far longer than human civilization has existed.
