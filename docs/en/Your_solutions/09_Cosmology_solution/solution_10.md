# Task 10 – Cosmic Microwave Background

## Problem Statement

The Cosmic Microwave Background radiation now has temperature

$$
T_{\text{now}} \approx 2.7 \text{ K}
$$

When it was emitted during recombination, it had temperature

$$
T_{\text{rec}} \approx 3000 \text{ K}
$$

Using Wien's displacement law,

$$
\lambda_{\text{peak}} = \frac{b}{T}
$$

with

$$
b \approx 2.898 \times 10^{-3} \text{ m K}
$$

determine the wavelength of peak intensity at recombination and today.

## Theory

Wien's law states that the peak wavelength of blackbody radiation is inversely proportional to temperature.

## Step-by-Step Solution

### 1. Peak wavelength at recombination

$$
\lambda_{\text{peak,rec}} = \frac{2.898 \times 10^{-3}}{3000}
$$

$$
\lambda_{\text{peak,rec}} = 9.66 \times 10^{-7} \text{ m}
$$

This is

$$
966 \text{ nm}
$$

### 2. Peak wavelength now

$$
\lambda_{\text{peak,now}} = \frac{2.898 \times 10^{-3}}{2.7}
$$

$$
\lambda_{\text{peak,now}} \approx 1.07 \times 10^{-3} \text{ m}
$$

This is

$$
1.07 \text{ mm}
$$

## Final Result

At recombination, the peak wavelength was approximately

$$
\lambda_{\text{peak,rec}} \approx 9.66 \times 10^{-7} \text{ m} = 966 \text{ nm}
$$

Today, the peak wavelength is approximately

$$
\lambda_{\text{peak,now}} \approx 1.07 \times 10^{-3} \text{ m} = 1.07 \text{ mm}
$$

## Interpretation

As the universe expanded, the CMB cooled and its peak wavelength shifted from the near-infrared region to the microwave region.
