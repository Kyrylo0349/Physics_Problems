# Task 01 – Cosmic Chronology

## Problem Statement

Place the following events of the early universe in chronological order, from earliest to latest:

- Recombination
- Big Bang Nucleosynthesis
- Inflation
- Formation of first galaxies
- Quark-gluon plasma era

## Theory

The early universe passed through a sequence of physical stages as it expanded and cooled. The correct order follows the decrease of temperature and density with time.

## Step-by-Step Solution

The sequence is:

1. **Inflation**
2. **Quark-gluon plasma era**
3. **Big Bang Nucleosynthesis**
4. **Recombination**
5. **Formation of first galaxies**

## Final Result

The chronological order is

$$
\text{Inflation} \rightarrow \text{Quark-gluon plasma era} \rightarrow \text{Big Bang Nucleosynthesis} \rightarrow \text{Recombination} \rightarrow \text{Formation of first galaxies}
$$

## Interpretation

Inflation occurred extremely early, followed by a very hot plasma state. Light atomic nuclei formed during nucleosynthesis, neutral atoms formed during recombination, and galaxies appeared much later after structure growth.
