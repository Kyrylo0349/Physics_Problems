# Task 07 – Galactic Trivia

## Problem Statement

Answer the following:

a) What is the Milky Way galaxy's ratio of diameter to thickness?

b) If $200$ billion stars in the Milky Way were distributed equally among the present Earth's people, how many stars would each person get?

c) How much time would it take to count all the stars in the Milky Way if you counted one star per second without stopping?

## Theory

Use simple order-of-magnitude estimates.

For part a), take:

- Milky Way diameter

$$
\approx 100000 \text{ light-years}
$$

- Milky Way disk thickness

$$
\approx 1000 \text{ light-years}
$$

For part b), use a present world population approximation of

$$
8.2 \times 10^9
$$

people.

## Step-by-Step Solution

### a) Diameter to thickness ratio

$$
\frac{100000}{1000} = 100
$$

So the ratio is

$$
100:1
$$

### b) Stars per person

$$
\frac{200 \times 10^9}{8.2 \times 10^9} \approx 24.39
$$

So each person would get about

$$
24
$$

stars.

### c) Counting time

At one star per second, the total number of seconds is

$$
200 \times 10^9 \text{ s}
$$

Convert to years:

$$
t = \frac{200 \times 10^9}{365.25 \times 24 \times 3600}
$$

$$
t \approx 6338 \text{ years}
$$

## Final Result

a) The Milky Way ratio of diameter to thickness is approximately

$$
100:1
$$

b) Each person would get about

$$
24
$$

stars.

c) Counting all the stars at one per second would take about

$$
6338 \text{ years}
$$

## Interpretation

The Milky Way is a very thin disk compared with its diameter. Its stellar population is so large that even sharing it among billions of people still gives dozens of stars per person.
