# Task 09 – Doppler Effect

## Problem Statement

A galaxy is receding from Earth at speed

$$
v = 5 \times 10^6 \text{ m/s}
$$

It emits light with wavelength

$$
\lambda_{\text{em}} = 486 \text{ nm}
$$

Determine the observed wavelength using:

1. the non-relativistic Doppler shift,

$$
\lambda_{\text{obs}} = \lambda_{\text{em}} \left(1 + \frac{v}{c}\right)
$$

2. the relativistic Doppler shift,

$$
\lambda_{\text{obs}} = \lambda_{\text{em}} \sqrt{\frac{1 + \frac{v}{c}}{1 - \frac{v}{c}}}
$$

Compare the results.

## Theory

For a receding source, the observed wavelength is larger than the emitted wavelength, so the light is redshifted.

## Step-by-Step Solution

Use

$$
c = 3.0 \times 10^8 \text{ m/s}
$$

Then

$$
\frac{v}{c} = \frac{5 \times 10^6}{3.0 \times 10^8} = \frac{1}{60} \approx 0.01667
$$

### 1. Non-relativistic result

$$
\lambda_{\text{obs}} = 486 \left(1 + 0.01667\right) \text{ nm}
$$

$$
\lambda_{\text{obs}} \approx 494.10 \text{ nm}
$$

### 2. Relativistic result

$$
\lambda_{\text{obs}} = 486 \sqrt{\frac{1 + 0.01667}{1 - 0.01667}} \text{ nm}
$$

$$
\lambda_{\text{obs}} \approx 494.17 \text{ nm}
$$

### 3. Difference

$$
\Delta \lambda \approx 494.17 - 494.10 = 0.07 \text{ nm}
$$

## Final Result

Non-relativistic Doppler shift:

$$
\lambda_{\text{obs}} \approx 494.10 \text{ nm}
$$

Relativistic Doppler shift:

$$
\lambda_{\text{obs}} \approx 494.17 \text{ nm}
$$

The difference is about

$$
0.07 \text{ nm}
$$

## Interpretation

At this speed, the relativistic correction is small but measurable in principle. Both methods predict a redshift because the galaxy is moving away.
