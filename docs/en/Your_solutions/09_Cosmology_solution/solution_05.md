# Task 05 – Galactic Collision

## Problem Statement

The Andromeda galaxy is about

$$
2.5 \times 10^6 \text{ light-years}
$$

away and is moving toward the Milky Way at about

$$
110 \text{ km/s}
$$

Estimate how long it will take until the two galaxies collide.

## Theory

Using a simple constant-speed estimate,

$$
t = \frac{d}{v}
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

Convert the distance to kilometers:

$$
d = 2.5 \times 10^6 \times 9.4607 \times 10^{12} \text{ km}
$$

$$
d \approx 2.37 \times 10^{19} \text{ km}
$$

Now divide by the speed:

$$
t = \frac{2.37 \times 10^{19}}{110} \text{ s}
$$

$$
t \approx 2.15 \times 10^{17} \text{ s}
$$

Convert to years:

$$
t \approx \frac{2.15 \times 10^{17}}{365.25 \times 24 \times 3600}
$$

$$
t \approx 6.81 \times 10^9 \text{ years}
$$

## Final Result

The estimated collision time is approximately

$$
t \approx 6.8 \times 10^9 \text{ years}
$$

or about

$$
6.8 \text{ billion years}
$$

## Interpretation

This is a simple order-of-magnitude estimate based on constant approach speed. Real galactic dynamics are more complicated, but the timescale is correct in rough terms.
