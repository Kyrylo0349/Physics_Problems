# Task 03 – Cosmic Sizes

## Problem Statement

Order the following objects from smallest to largest:

- Earth
- Milky Way galaxy
- Solar System
- observable universe
- Sun
- Andromeda galaxy
- human
- Moon
- Pluto
- Jupiter
- Ceres
- Local Group of galaxies

## Theory

This is a scale-comparison problem. The ordering should follow typical physical size or spatial extent.

## Step-by-Step Solution

From smallest to largest:

1. human
2. Ceres
3. Pluto
4. Moon
5. Earth
6. Jupiter
7. Sun
8. Solar System
9. Milky Way galaxy
10. Andromeda galaxy
11. Local Group of galaxies
12. observable universe

## Final Result

The order from smallest to largest is

$$
\text{human} \rightarrow \text{Ceres} \rightarrow \text{Pluto} \rightarrow \text{Moon} \rightarrow \text{Earth} \rightarrow \text{Jupiter} \rightarrow \text{Sun} \rightarrow \text{Solar System} \rightarrow \text{Milky Way} \rightarrow \text{Andromeda} \rightarrow \text{Local Group} \rightarrow \text{observable universe}
$$

## Interpretation

Planetary bodies differ by orders of magnitude, stars are much larger than planets, galaxies are vastly larger than stellar systems, and galaxy groups and the observable universe are larger still.
