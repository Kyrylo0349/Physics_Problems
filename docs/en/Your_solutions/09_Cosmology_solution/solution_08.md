# Task 08 – Cosmic Expansion

## Problem Statement

If the universe is expanding at a rate of

$$
70 \text{ km/s per Mpc}
$$

how much will the distance between two galaxies that are currently

$$
100 \text{ million light-years}
$$

apart increase after

$$
1 \text{ billion years}
$$

Assume that the expansion rate remains constant over this time period.

Use

$$
1 \text{ Mpc} \approx 3.26 \text{ million light-years}
$$

## Theory

Hubble's law gives the recession speed:

$$
v = H_0 d
$$

If the recession speed is treated as constant over the interval, then the increase in distance is

$$
\Delta d = vt
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

### 1. Convert the distance to Mpc

$$
d = \frac{100}{3.26} \text{ Mpc}
$$

$$
d \approx 30.67 \text{ Mpc}
$$

### 2. Recession speed

$$
v = 70 \cdot 30.67 \text{ km/s}
$$

$$
v \approx 2147 \text{ km/s}
$$

### 3. Time interval

$$
t = 1 \text{ billion years} = 10^9 \times 365.25 \times 24 \times 3600 \text{ s}
$$

$$
t \approx 3.156 \times 10^{16} \text{ s}
$$

### 4. Increase in distance

$$
\Delta d = vt
$$

$$
\Delta d \approx 2147 \times 3.156 \times 10^{16} \text{ km}
$$

$$
\Delta d \approx 6.78 \times 10^{19} \text{ km}
$$

Convert to light-years using

$$
1 \text{ ly} \approx 9.4607 \times 10^{12} \text{ km}
$$

$$
\Delta d \approx \frac{6.78 \times 10^{19}}{9.4607 \times 10^{12}} \text{ ly}
$$

$$
\Delta d \approx 7.16 \times 10^6 \text{ ly}
$$

## Final Result

The distance increases by approximately

$$
7.16 \times 10^6 \text{ light-years}
$$

or about

$$
7.2 \text{ million light-years}
$$

in 1 billion years.

## Interpretation

This is a simple constant-rate estimate. It gives the correct scale of the effect, although the real cosmic expansion history is more complicated.
