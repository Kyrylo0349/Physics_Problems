# Task 02 – Orbital Mechanics

## Problem Statement

Calculate the orbital speed of the International Space Station, which orbits at altitude

$$
h = 400 \text{ km}
$$

above the Earth's surface.

Use

$$
M_E \approx 5.97 \times 10^{24} \text{ kg}
$$

Compare this with the Earth's orbital speed around the Sun, using

$$
r_{ES} \approx 150 \times 10^6 \text{ km}
$$

and

$$
T = 365.25 \text{ days}
$$

Determine which is faster.

## Theory

For a circular orbit around the Earth,

$$
v = \sqrt{\frac{GM_E}{r}}
$$

where

$$
r = R_E + h
$$

For Earth's orbit around the Sun, assuming a circular orbit,

$$
v = \frac{2\pi r}{T}
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

### 1. ISS orbital speed

Take

$$
R_E = 6378 \text{ km}
$$

Then the orbital radius is

$$
r = 6378 + 400 = 6778 \text{ km} = 6.778 \times 10^6 \text{ m}
$$

Use

$$
v_{\text{ISS}} = \sqrt{\frac{GM_E}{r}}
$$

with

$$
G = 6.67 \times 10^{-11} \text{ N m}^2 \text{/kg}^2
$$

Substituting gives

$$
v_{\text{ISS}} \approx 7.67 \times 10^3 \text{ m/s}
$$

$$
v_{\text{ISS}} \approx 7.67 \text{ km/s}
$$

### 2. Earth's orbital speed around the Sun

Use

$$
r_{ES} = 150 \times 10^6 \text{ km}
$$

and

$$
T = 365.25 \cdot 24 \cdot 3600 \text{ s}
$$

Then

$$
v_E = \frac{2\pi r_{ES}}{T}
$$

Substituting gives

$$
v_E \approx 29.9 \text{ km/s}
$$

## Final Result

The ISS orbital speed is approximately

$$
v_{\text{ISS}} \approx 7.67 \text{ km/s}
$$

The Earth's orbital speed around the Sun is approximately

$$
v_E \approx 29.9 \text{ km/s}
$$

Therefore, the Earth in its orbit around the Sun is faster.

## Interpretation

Although the ISS moves very quickly around the Earth, the Earth moves even faster around the Sun because the orbital radius is enormous and the orbital path is much larger.
