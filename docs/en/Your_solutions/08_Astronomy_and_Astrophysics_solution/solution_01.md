# Task 01 – Rotational Velocity at the Earth's Equator

## Problem Statement

Calculate the linear speed of a point on the Earth's equator due to the Earth's rotation.

Use

$$
R_E \approx 6378 \text{ km}
$$

## Theory

A point on the equator moves along a circle of radius $R_E$ in one full day. The linear speed is

$$
v = \frac{2\pi R_E}{T}
$$

For one rotation, take

$$
T = 24 \text{ h} = 86400 \text{ s}
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

The circumference of the equator is

$$
2\pi R_E = 2\pi \cdot 6378 \text{ km}
$$

$$
2\pi R_E \approx 40074 \text{ km}
$$

Now divide by the rotation period:

$$
v = \frac{40074}{86400} \text{ km/s}
$$

$$
v \approx 0.464 \text{ km/s}
$$

## Final Result

The linear speed at the Earth's equator is

$$
v \approx 0.464 \text{ km/s}
$$

## Interpretation

Points on the equator move fastest because they are farthest from the Earth's rotation axis. Points closer to the poles have smaller linear speed.
