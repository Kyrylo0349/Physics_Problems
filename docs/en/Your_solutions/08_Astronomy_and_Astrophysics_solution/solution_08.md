# Task 08 – Interplanetary Travel Time

## Problem Statement

Assume the closest Earth–Mars distance is

$$
d = 55 \times 10^6 \text{ km}
$$

Determine the travel time:

- a) for a message moving at the speed of light,
- b) for a spacecraft moving at

$$
40000 \text{ km/h}
$$

- c) for an airplane moving at

$$
900 \text{ km/h}
$$

## Theory

For constant speed motion,

$$
t = \frac{d}{v}
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

### a) Message at the speed of light

Use

$$
c \approx 3.00 \times 10^5 \text{ km/s}
$$

Then

$$
t = \frac{55 \times 10^6}{3.00 \times 10^5} \text{ s}
$$

$$
t \approx 183 \text{ s}
$$

This is about

$$
3.05 \text{ min}
$$

### b) Spacecraft at 40000 km/h

$$
t = \frac{55 \times 10^6}{40000} \text{ h}
$$

$$
t = 1375 \text{ h}
$$

Convert to days:

$$
t = \frac{1375}{24} \approx 57.3 \text{ days}
$$

### c) Airplane at 900 km/h

$$
t = \frac{55 \times 10^6}{900} \text{ h}
$$

$$
t \approx 61111 \text{ h}
$$

Convert to days and years:

$$
t \approx \frac{61111}{24} \approx 2546 \text{ days}
$$

$$
t \approx 6.97 \text{ years}
$$

## Final Result

a) At the speed of light:

$$
t \approx 183 \text{ s} \approx 3.05 \text{ min}
$$

b) At 40000 km/h:

$$
t \approx 1375 \text{ h} \approx 57.3 \text{ days}
$$

c) At 900 km/h:

$$
t \approx 61111 \text{ h} \approx 2546 \text{ days} \approx 6.97 \text{ years}
$$

## Interpretation

Even at the closest approach, interplanetary distances are enormous. Electromagnetic signals arrive in minutes, but ordinary transportation speeds make the trip extremely long.
