# Task 06 – Solar Gravity

## Problem Statement

Calculate the gravitational acceleration on the surface of the Sun.

Use

$$
M_S \approx 2.0 \times 10^{30} \text{ kg}
$$

$$
R_S \approx 6.96 \times 10^8 \text{ m}
$$

Determine by what factor your weight would increase compared with the Earth.

## Theory

Surface gravity is

$$
g = \frac{GM}{R^2}
$$

The factor by which weight changes is

$$
\frac{g_{\text{Sun}}}{g_{\text{Earth}}}
$$

with

$$
g_{\text{Earth}} \approx 9.81 \text{ m/s}^2
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

Compute

$$
g_{\text{Sun}} = \frac{GM_S}{R_S^2}
$$

Using

$$
G = 6.67 \times 10^{-11}
$$

$$
M_S = 2.0 \times 10^{30} \text{ kg}
$$

$$
R_S = 6.96 \times 10^8 \text{ m}
$$

gives

$$
g_{\text{Sun}} \approx 275.6 \text{ m/s}^2
$$

Now compare with Earth:

$$
\frac{g_{\text{Sun}}}{g_{\text{Earth}}} = \frac{275.6}{9.81} \approx 28.1
$$

## Final Result

The gravitational acceleration at the Sun's surface is approximately

$$
g_{\text{Sun}} \approx 276 \text{ m/s}^2
$$

Your weight would be about

$$
28.1
$$

times larger than on Earth.

## Interpretation

The Sun is extremely massive, so even though it is also very large, its surface gravity remains much stronger than the Earth's.
