# Task 05 – Escape Velocity from the Moon

## Problem Statement

Calculate the escape velocity from the surface of the Moon.

Use

$$
M_M \approx 7.35 \times 10^{22} \text{ kg}
$$

$$
R_M \approx 1737 \text{ km}
$$

Express the answer in km/s and as a fraction of the Earth's escape velocity

$$
11.2 \text{ km/s}
$$

## Theory

Escape velocity from a spherical body is

$$
v_{\text{esc}} = \sqrt{\frac{2GM}{R}}
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

Convert the Moon's radius to meters:

$$
R_M = 1737 \text{ km} = 1.737 \times 10^6 \text{ m}
$$

Now calculate

$$
v_{\text{esc}} = \sqrt{\frac{2GM_M}{R_M}}
$$

Using

$$
G = 6.67 \times 10^{-11}
$$

and

$$
M_M = 7.35 \times 10^{22} \text{ kg}
$$

gives

$$
v_{\text{esc}} \approx 2.38 \times 10^3 \text{ m/s}
$$

$$
v_{\text{esc}} \approx 2.38 \text{ km/s}
$$

Now compare with Earth:

$$
\frac{v_{\text{esc,Moon}}}{v_{\text{esc,Earth}}} = \frac{2.38}{11.2} \approx 0.212
$$

## Final Result

The escape velocity from the Moon is approximately

$$
v_{\text{esc}} \approx 2.38 \text{ km/s}
$$

As a fraction of the Earth's escape velocity, this is

$$
0.212
$$

or about

$$
21.2\%
$$

## Interpretation

The Moon's escape velocity is much smaller than the Earth's because the Moon is both less massive and much smaller.
