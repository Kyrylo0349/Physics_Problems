# Task 07 – Dyson Sphere Radius

## Problem Statement

Suppose the mass of Mercury,

$$
M = 3.3 \times 10^{23} \text{ kg}
$$

is used to build a spherical solar-panel shell with surface density

$$
\sigma = 10 \text{ kg/m}^2
$$

Determine the radius of the sphere.

## Theory

The mass of a thin spherical shell is

$$
M = \sigma A
$$

where the surface area of a sphere is

$$
A = 4\pi R^2
$$

Thus,

$$
M = 4\pi R^2 \sigma
$$

and therefore

$$
R = \sqrt{\frac{M}{4\pi \sigma}}
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

Substitute the values:

$$
R = \sqrt{\frac{3.3 \times 10^{23}}{4\pi \cdot 10}}
$$

This gives

$$
R \approx 5.12 \times 10^{10} \text{ m}
$$

Convert to kilometers:

$$
R \approx 5.12 \times 10^7 \text{ km}
$$

## Final Result

The radius of the Dyson sphere would be approximately

$$
R \approx 5.12 \times 10^{10} \text{ m}
$$

or

$$
R \approx 5.12 \times 10^7 \text{ km}
$$

## Interpretation

This radius is much smaller than the Earth's orbit around the Sun, but still enormous on planetary scales.
