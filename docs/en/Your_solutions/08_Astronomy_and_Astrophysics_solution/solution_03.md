# Task 03 – Microgravity at the ISS Altitude

## Problem Statement

Determine the acceleration due to gravity at the altitude of the ISS,

$$
h = 400 \text{ km}
$$

Explain why astronauts experience weightlessness despite the presence of gravity.

## Theory

The gravitational acceleration at distance $r$ from the Earth's center is

$$
g(r) = \frac{GM_E}{r^2}
$$

with

$$
r = R_E + h
$$

Weightlessness in orbit does not mean gravity is absent. It means the spacecraft and astronauts are in continuous free fall together.

## Step-by-Step Solution

Use

$$
R_E = 6378 \text{ km}
$$

so

$$
r = 6378 + 400 = 6778 \text{ km} = 6.778 \times 10^6 \text{ m}
$$

Now calculate

$$
g_{\text{ISS}} = \frac{GM_E}{r^2}
$$

Using

$$
G = 6.67 \times 10^{-11}
$$

and

$$
M_E = 5.97 \times 10^{24} \text{ kg}
$$

gives

$$
g_{\text{ISS}} \approx 8.67 \text{ m/s}^2
$$

This is about

$$
\frac{8.67}{9.81} \approx 0.88
$$

of the surface value.

## Final Result

The gravitational acceleration at the ISS altitude is approximately

$$
g_{\text{ISS}} \approx 8.67 \text{ m/s}^2
$$

Astronauts feel weightless because they and the ISS are both in free fall around the Earth.

## Interpretation

Gravity at the ISS is still strong. The sensation of weightlessness comes from the absence of a supporting normal force, not from the absence of gravity.
