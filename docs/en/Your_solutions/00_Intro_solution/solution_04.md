# Task 04 – Rearranging the Pendulum Formula

## Problem Statement

The period of a simple pendulum is given by

$$
T = 2\pi \sqrt{\frac{L}{g}}
$$

Rearrange the formula to obtain an expression for $g$.

## Theory

Rearranging a formula means isolating the required variable by applying inverse operations in a correct algebraic order. In this expression, the operations include multiplication, division, a square root, and squaring.

## Step-by-Step Solution

Start with

$$
T = 2\pi \sqrt{\frac{L}{g}}
$$

Divide both sides by $2\pi$:

$$
\frac{T}{2\pi} = \sqrt{\frac{L}{g}}
$$

Square both sides to remove the square root:

$$
\left( \frac{T}{2\pi} \right)^2 = \frac{L}{g}
$$

Expand the square on the left-hand side:

$$
\frac{T^2}{4\pi^2} = \frac{L}{g}
$$

Multiply both sides by $g$:

$$
g \frac{T^2}{4\pi^2} = L
$$

Now multiply both sides by

$$
\frac{4\pi^2}{T^2}
$$

to isolate $g$:

$$
g = \frac{4\pi^2 L}{T^2}
$$

## Final Result

$$
g = \frac{4\pi^2 L}{T^2}
$$

## Interpretation

This rearranged form shows that the gravitational acceleration can be determined experimentally by measuring the pendulum length $L$ and its period $T$. It also shows that $g$ increases with $L$ and decreases with the square of the period.






# Task 04 – Mini Theory: Rearranging Formulas

## What this topic is about

Rearranging a formula means solving an equation for a different variable. The physical law stays the same, but its form is changed to make one chosen quantity the subject.

For example, the pendulum formula is

$$
T = 2\pi \sqrt{\frac{L}{g}}
$$

## Key ideas

Algebraic operations must be applied equally to both sides of the equation.

Square roots are removed by squaring.

Fractions are removed by multiplication.

The goal is to isolate one variable clearly.

## Why it matters in physics

Physics formulas are often used in different ways depending on what is known and what must be found. Rearranging formulas is essential in laboratory work, problem solving, and deriving new relations.
