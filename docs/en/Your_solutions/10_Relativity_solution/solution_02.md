# Task 02 – Time Dilation

## Problem Statement

Two identical devices are synchronized on Earth. One remains on Earth, while the other travels on a spaceship at relativistic speed.

Determine the spaceship speed if the Earth device has played 2 one-hour episodes while the spaceship device has played only 1 one-hour episode.

## Theory

Time dilation is described by

$$
\Delta t = \gamma \Delta \tau
$$

where:

- $\Delta \tau$ is the proper time measured by the moving device,
- $\Delta t$ is the time measured in the Earth frame.

## Step-by-Step Solution

The Earth device measures

$$
\Delta t = 2 \text{ h}
$$

The spaceship device measures

$$
\Delta \tau = 1 \text{ h}
$$

Thus,

$$
\gamma = \frac{\Delta t}{\Delta \tau} = \frac{2}{1} = 2
$$

Now solve for the speed:

$$
2 = \frac{1}{\sqrt{1 - \frac{v^2}{c^2}}}
$$

$$
\sqrt{1 - \frac{v^2}{c^2}} = \frac{1}{2}
$$

$$
1 - \frac{v^2}{c^2} = \frac{1}{4}
$$

$$
\frac{v^2}{c^2} = \frac{3}{4}
$$

$$
\frac{v}{c} \approx 0.866
$$

## Final Result

The spaceship must move at

$$
v \approx 0.866c
$$

## Interpretation

For the spaceship clock to run at half the Earth rate, the Lorentz factor must be

$$
\gamma = 2
$$

which corresponds to a speed of about

$$
86.6\%
$$

of the speed of light.
