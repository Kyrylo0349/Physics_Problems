# Task 05 – Relativistic Mass

## Problem Statement

Determine the relativistic mass of a

$$
50 \text{ kg}
$$

person traveling at

$$
0.999c
$$

## Theory

Using the older relativistic-mass notation,

$$
m_{\text{rel}} = \gamma m_0
$$

where

$$
\gamma = \frac{1}{\sqrt{1 - \frac{v^2}{c^2}}}
$$

and

$$
m_0
$$

is the rest mass.

## Step-by-Step Solution

For

$$
v = 0.999c
$$

the Lorentz factor is

$$
\gamma = \frac{1}{\sqrt{1 - 0.999^2}} \approx 22.37
$$

Now calculate the relativistic mass:

$$
m_{\text{rel}} = \gamma m_0
$$

$$
m_{\text{rel}} \approx 22.37 \cdot 50
$$

$$
m_{\text{rel}} \approx 1118 \text{ kg}
$$

## Final Result

The relativistic mass is approximately

$$
m_{\text{rel}} \approx 1.12 \times 10^3 \text{ kg}
$$

## Interpretation

In modern relativity, it is more common to keep mass invariant and describe the increase through total energy and momentum. In the older terminology, the relativistic mass becomes much larger at speeds extremely close to

$$
c
$$
