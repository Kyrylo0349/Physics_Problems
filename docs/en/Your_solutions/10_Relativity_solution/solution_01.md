# Task 01 – Length Contraction

## Problem Statement

A ruler has proper length

$$
L_0 = 1.0 \text{ m}
$$

At what speed must it move so that its observed length is

$$
L = 0.5 \text{ m}
$$

due to relativistic length contraction?

## Theory

Length contraction is described by

$$
L = \frac{L_0}{\gamma}
$$

where

$$
\gamma = \frac{1}{\sqrt{1 - \frac{v^2}{c^2}}}
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

From

$$
L = \frac{L_0}{\gamma}
$$

substitute the given values:

$$
0.5 = \frac{1.0}{\gamma}
$$

Thus,

$$
\gamma = 2
$$

Now solve for the speed:

$$
2 = \frac{1}{\sqrt{1 - \frac{v^2}{c^2}}}
$$

$$
\sqrt{1 - \frac{v^2}{c^2}} = \frac{1}{2}
$$

$$
1 - \frac{v^2}{c^2} = \frac{1}{4}
$$

$$
\frac{v^2}{c^2} = \frac{3}{4}
$$

$$
\frac{v}{c} = \sqrt{\frac{3}{4}} \approx 0.866
$$

## Final Result

The ruler must move at

$$
v \approx 0.866c
$$

## Interpretation

A contraction by a factor of 2 corresponds to

$$
\gamma = 2
$$

which requires a speed very close to the speed of light.
