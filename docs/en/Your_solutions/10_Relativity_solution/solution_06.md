# Task 06 – Mass-Energy

## Problem Statement

An atomic bomb converts approximately

$$
0.7 \text{ g}
$$

of mass into energy.

Using

$$
E = mc^2
$$

calculate the released energy in joules and in kilotons of TNT.

Use

$$
1 \text{ kiloton TNT} \approx 4.184 \times 10^{12} \text{ J}
$$

## Theory

Mass-energy equivalence is

$$
E = mc^2
$$

The converted mass must be expressed in kilograms.

## Step-by-Step Solution

Convert the mass:

$$
m = 0.7 \text{ g} = 0.0007 \text{ kg}
$$

Now apply

$$
E = mc^2
$$

with

$$
c = 2.99792458 \times 10^8 \text{ m/s}
$$

Then

$$
E = 0.0007 \cdot c^2
$$

$$
E \approx 6.29 \times 10^{13} \text{ J}
$$

Now convert to kilotons of TNT:

$$
E_{\text{kt}} = \frac{6.29 \times 10^{13}}{4.184 \times 10^{12}}
$$

$$
E_{\text{kt}} \approx 15.0
$$

## Final Result

The released energy is approximately

$$
E \approx 6.29 \times 10^{13} \text{ J}
$$

This corresponds to about

$$
15.0 \text{ kilotons of TNT}
$$

## Interpretation

A very small amount of mass corresponds to an enormous amount of energy because of the factor

$$
c^2
$$

in the mass-energy relation.
