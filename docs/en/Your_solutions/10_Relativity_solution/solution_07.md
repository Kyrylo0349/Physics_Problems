# Task 07 – Velocity Transformation

## Problem Statement

A rocket moves away from Earth at

$$
0.8c
$$

It launches a probe forward at

$$
0.5c
$$

relative to the rocket.

Determine the probe speed as measured from Earth:

- according to classical mechanics,
- according to special relativity.

## Theory

In classical mechanics, velocities add directly:

$$
u = u' + v
$$

In special relativity, velocities combine according to

$$
u = \frac{u' + v}{1 + \frac{u'v}{c^2}}
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

### 1. Classical result

$$
u_{\text{classical}} = 0.8c + 0.5c = 1.3c
$$

### 2. Relativistic result

Use

$$
u = \frac{0.8c + 0.5c}{1 + (0.8)(0.5)}
$$

$$
u = \frac{1.3c}{1.4}
$$

$$
u \approx 0.9286c
$$

## Final Result

According to classical mechanics,

$$
u_{\text{classical}} = 1.3c
$$

According to special relativity,

$$
u_{\text{rel}} \approx 0.9286c
$$

## Interpretation

Classical addition incorrectly predicts a speed greater than the speed of light. Special relativity prevents this and always gives a result smaller than

$$
c
$$

for material objects.
