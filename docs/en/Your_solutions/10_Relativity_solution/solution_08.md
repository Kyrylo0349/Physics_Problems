# Task 08 – Adding Velocities

## Problem Statement

What happens to the relative velocities of two objects moving away from each other if one of the objects is moving at the speed of light?

## Theory

In special relativity, the velocity-addition formula is

$$
u = \frac{u' + v}{1 + \frac{u'v}{c^2}}
$$

If one of the objects is light, then its speed is

$$
c
$$

in every inertial reference frame.

## Step-by-Step Solution

Suppose one object has speed

$$
u' = c
$$

and another frame moves at speed

$$
v
$$

Then

$$
u = \frac{c + v}{1 + \frac{cv}{c^2}}
$$

$$
u = \frac{c + v}{1 + \frac{v}{c}}
$$

Multiply numerator and denominator by

$$
c
$$

to simplify:

$$
u = \frac{c(c+v)}{c+v} = c
$$

So if one object is light, every inertial observer still measures its speed to be

$$
c
$$

## Final Result

If one of the objects is moving at the speed of light, its relative speed remains

$$
c
$$

for all inertial observers.

## Interpretation

Special relativity makes the speed of light invariant. No inertial observer measures light to move faster than

$$
c
$$

or slower than

$$
c
$$

.
