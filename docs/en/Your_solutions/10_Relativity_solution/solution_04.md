# Task 04 – High-Energy Physics

## Problem Statement

How much energy is required to accelerate a proton from rest to speed

$$
v = 0.99c
$$

The proton rest mass is approximately

$$
938 \text{ MeV}/c^2
$$

## Theory

The total relativistic energy is

$$
E = \gamma mc^2
$$

The kinetic energy required to accelerate the proton from rest is

$$
K = (\gamma - 1)mc^2
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

For

$$
v = 0.99c
$$

the Lorentz factor is

$$
\gamma = \frac{1}{\sqrt{1 - 0.99^2}} \approx 7.089
$$

The proton rest energy is

$$
mc^2 = 938 \text{ MeV}
$$

Thus the kinetic energy is

$$
K = (\gamma - 1)mc^2
$$

$$
K = (7.089 - 1)\cdot 938 \text{ MeV}
$$

$$
K \approx 5711 \text{ MeV}
$$

$$
K \approx 5.71 \text{ GeV}
$$

For reference, the total energy is

$$
E = \gamma mc^2 \approx 6649 \text{ MeV} \approx 6.65 \text{ GeV}
$$

## Final Result

The required kinetic energy is approximately

$$
K \approx 5.71 \text{ GeV}
$$

The corresponding total relativistic energy is approximately

$$
E \approx 6.65 \text{ GeV}
$$

## Interpretation

At

$$
0.99c
$$

the proton is already deeply in the relativistic regime, so the required kinetic energy is many times larger than its rest energy.
