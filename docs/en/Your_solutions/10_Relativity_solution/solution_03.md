# Task 03 – Time Dilation in Real Life

## Problem Statement

A muon has average lifetime

$$
\tau_0 = 2.20\,\mu\text{s}
$$

in its rest frame.

If it travels at

$$
v = 0.990c
$$

what is its average lifetime as measured on Earth?

## Theory

A moving clock runs slower, so the lifetime measured in the Earth frame is

$$
\tau = \gamma \tau_0
$$

with

$$
\gamma = \frac{1}{\sqrt{1 - \frac{v^2}{c^2}}}
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

For

$$
v = 0.990c
$$

the Lorentz factor is

$$
\gamma = \frac{1}{\sqrt{1 - 0.990^2}}
$$

$$
\gamma = \frac{1}{\sqrt{1 - 0.9801}} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{0.0199}} \approx 7.09
$$

Now calculate the dilated lifetime:

$$
\tau = \gamma \tau_0
$$

$$
\tau \approx 7.09 \cdot 2.20\,\mu\text{s}
$$

$$
\tau \approx 15.6\,\mu\text{s}
$$

## Final Result

The average lifetime measured on Earth is

$$
\tau \approx 15.6\,\mu\text{s}
$$

## Interpretation

Because the muon moves extremely fast, its internal clock appears slowed down in the Earth frame, so it survives much longer than in its own rest frame.
