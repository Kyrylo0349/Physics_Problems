# Task 09 – Twin Paradox for the ISS

## Problem Statement

An astronaut spends one year on the International Space Station, which moves at about

$$
v = 7.66 \text{ km/s}
$$

Determine how much younger the astronaut is than a twin on Earth due only to kinetic time dilation.

## Theory

For speeds much smaller than

$$
c
$$

the kinetic time-dilation difference over time

$$
t
$$

is approximately

$$
\Delta t \approx \frac{v^2}{2c^2} t
$$

This gives the amount by which the moving clock lags behind the Earth clock.

## Step-by-Step Solution

Convert the speed:

$$
v = 7.66 \text{ km/s} = 7660 \text{ m/s}
$$

Take one year as

$$
t = 365.25 \times 24 \times 3600 \text{ s}
$$

Now compute

$$
\Delta t \approx \frac{v^2}{2c^2} t
$$

Substituting the values gives

$$
\Delta t \approx 0.0103 \text{ s}
$$

## Final Result

Due only to kinetic time dilation, the astronaut is younger by approximately

$$
0.0103 \text{ s}
$$

or about

$$
10.3 \text{ ms}
$$

after one year.

## Interpretation

The ISS speed is tiny compared with the speed of light, so the relativistic effect is small, but still measurable in principle.
